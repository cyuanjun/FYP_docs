02 · Logo system
────────────────────────────────

The mark.

Mark concept

Three stacked bars — ascending left to right — read simultaneously as a leaderboard rank chart, a signal-strength indicator showing connection to others, and an upward trajectory. The tallest bar is lime; the others are cut versions of it. The whole thing sits inside the brand's signature clipped square.

This is not a heartbeat. Heartbeats belong to every other fitness app. This is a scoreboard. That's what PulseUp actually is.

Mark anatomy
Rank bars
3 ascending bars = leaderboard positions. Reads as signal strength (social connectivity) and upward trajectory simultaneously.
Tallest bar = #1
Full opacity. The goal state. What every user is working toward.
Corner notch echo
The brand clip-path detail mirrors itself inside the mark — a small cut on the tallest bar's top-right corner. Invisible at small sizes, a reward detail at large.
Icon sizes

Rules

Never scale below 20px. At 28px and below, suppress the corner notch detail — it will not render cleanly. Never round the outer icon corners. Never distort bar proportions — the 3-bar ascending ratio (37% · 63% · 100% height) must be preserved. Never replace the three-bar mark with any other chart-like shape. Minimum clearspace = half the icon height on all sides.

Accessibility — contrast requirements
BODY TEXT (≥14px)
Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio required. Use --muted (#8A8A84) or brighter on --bg2. Never use --faint (#333330) for text.
LARGE TEXT (≥18px bold)
Minimum 3:1 contrast ratio. Display headlines in --ink or --lime on dark surfaces always pass. Outline text (stroke only) does not count as text for contrast.
INTERACTIVE ELEMENTS
All buttons, links and focusable elements must meet 3:1 against adjacent color. Lime CTAs on dark bg: ✓ passes. Add outline-offset:3px for keyboard focus rings.

05 · Brand voice & tone
────────────────────────────────

How we speak.

Energetic — sessions & streaks
YOU CRUSHED IT. 3-DAY STREAK. KEEP THE FIRE BURNING.
Direct, punchy, all-caps in UI headings. Celebrate loudly. No hedging, no disclaimers. Maximum 12 words for achievement copy. Think: hype man at mile 5 of a 10K.
Competitive — battles & leaderboard
JAI IS 840 STEPS AHEAD. 2 HOURS LEFT. CLOSE THE GAP.
Urgent, tactical, peer-pressure without cruelty. Use rival's name. Use exact numbers. Use a countdown. Never soften competition — lean into the tension.
Intelligent — coaching & data
"Your Zone 3 output is up 14%. Time to test your threshold."
Sentence case for AI coach copy. Data-first, specific, credible. Sound like the smartest trainer in the room. No jargon without a number to back it up.
Caring — rest & recovery
"6 days straight. Your body is asking for a rest day — let's schedule one."
Warm and direct. Never judgmental. We protect performance long-term. Sentence case — recovery moments feel different from competition moments.
Do
  • →Use real numbers always ("burned 487 kcal", not "calories burned")
  • →ALL-CAPS for achievement headlines and battle copy
  • →Use the user's rival's name in competition nudges
  • →Keep in-app battle copy under 12 words
  • →Switch to sentence case for coaching and recovery voice
Don't
  • ✗Use shame or guilt — channel it into competition instead
  • ✗Soften competition with phrases like "you're almost there!"
  • ✗Over-explain — if it can't be said in one sentence, it's too long
  • ✗Use emoji in UI copy — we're a performance brand, not casual
  • ✗Generalise health advice — always tie recommendations to data

Motion principles

No bounce. No rubber-band. No playful easing. This is a performance platform — motion should feel precise and athletic, like a sprinter's stride: explosive entry, clean stop. Reserve celebration animations (particle bursts, scale pops) exclusively for battle wins and goal completions — never for routine interactions.

Neon Forge corner rule

The angled clip-path on the logo icon and primary buttons (cutting the top-right corner) is a signature brand detail. Use clip-path: polygon(0 0, calc(100% - Npx) 0, 100% Npx, 100% 100%, 0 100%) consistently. N = 8px for small components, 10px for medium, 14px for large. Never mix rounded corners and clipped corners on the same component.
